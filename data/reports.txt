Note:
  - the Odroid C2 is cheating, it's not 2.016 GHz but 1.536. Frequencies of
    1536, 1752 and 2016 all produce exactly the same performance. It explains
    why this device is so slow compared to others when taking the frequency
    into account.

  - the cortex A9 and A17 don't always show the whole perf on 32kB, it seems
    like the physical address has a strong impact on the cache performance.

Note for CS008:
  - the CPU frequency has a proportional impact on L1 and L2 speed
  - A 21% increase of memory frequency only improves transaction rate by ~11%
  - the numbers remain the same per thread for 4 processes up to 32kB
  - at 256kB they're approx cut in half (113 instead of 165)
  - at 512kB they're cut by 6 (29 instead of 165)
  - at 1024 they're cut /8 (20 for 158)
  - at 2048k and above, the CPU frequency has no effect (L2 cache = 1M)
  - above 256kB, the results are much worse in multi-process than what
    they should be for only the same area split in 4. Maybe the memory
    controller is not a very good arbiter.
